Lasagna

Lasagna's greatness

Lasagna is amazing because it brings together so many comforting flavors and textures. The soft layers of pasta, rich sauce, creamy ricotta, melty mozzarella, and savory fillings create the perfect bite every time. Those crispy edges give it a satisfying crunch, while the cheesy, hearty layers make it feel extra indulgent. What really makes it special, though, is the care and effort that goes into making it—it’s more than just food, it’s a little slice of love.

Ingredients

  • ground beef/Italian sausage
  • onion
  • garlic
  • 1/4 cup of red wine
  • marinara sauce
  • dried thyme
  • sugar
  • parsley
  • lasagna noodles
  • ricotta cheese
  • cottage cheese
  • mozzarella cheese
  • egg

Steps:

  1. Brown the beef
  2. Create the sauce: Add the wine to a pan and stir until almost evaporated. Add marinara sauce, salt, pepper, thyme, sugar, and parsley. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook the sauce for 5 minutes
  3. Make the cheese filling: Combine cheese sauce ingredients and 1 cup mozzarella cheese in a mixing bowl (save the rest for later)
  4. Prep: Preheat oven to 375 degrees fahrenheit, cook lasagna noodles in well-salted water until al dente. Drain and pour in cold water.
  5. Assemble lasagna in a 9x13 inch casserole dish. Layer by first adding the meat, followed by 3 noodles, then 1/3 of the meat sauce, then 1 cup mozzarella, then half of the ricotta mixture. Repeat, then for the final layer, add 3 noodles, 1/3 meat sauce, and the rest of the mozzarella.
  6. Cover with aluminum foil, then bake at 375 degrees for 45 minutes. Remove foil and broil for 3-5 minutes, or until the cheese is slightly brown.
  7. Let lasagna rest for 30 minutes prior to cutting and serving.
